## The model in one paragraph

A group of like-minded people **pool their cash** and **buy a rental property outright — with no
debt** — through a shared legal structure (a company acting as trustee of a **unit trust**). Ownership
is split into a fixed number of **units** — say **1,000** — allocated in proportion to what each
person put in. The rent flows into a bank account the trust owns; insurance and maintenance come out
of it; and each period, everything above a kept "kitty" is **distributed to unit-holders by their
holding.** Start with **one** property, prove it works, then repeat.

## Why it works

- **No debt means very low risk.** As long as you only invest what you put in, there's no mortgage to
  default on. The asset is owned outright.
- **It opens the property market** to people who don't have $100k+ in cash lying around. A unit can be
  small — so ordinary people can own a real slice of real property.
- **It takes a home *out* of the speculative market.** The property becomes stable, reliable rental
  housing rather than something flipped for profit.
- **Income comes from rent, not resale.** The return is the ongoing rental yield — modest and solid,
  not a get-rich-quick play.

This is the cooperative spirit applied to housing: a way to provide both a fair return for everyday
investors *and* secure, affordable homes — at the same time.
